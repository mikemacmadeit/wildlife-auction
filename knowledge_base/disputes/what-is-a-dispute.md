# What is a Dispute?

A dispute is a formal way to report problems with your purchase and seek resolution.

## When to Open a Dispute

- **Item not as described** - Significant differences from listing
- **Item not received** - Delivery issues or non-delivery
- **Damaged item** - Damage during shipping or delivery
- **Seller unresponsive** - Seller won't communicate or resolve issues
- **Other problems** - Issues that can't be resolved with seller

## When NOT to Open a Dispute

- **Minor differences** - Small variations from photos
- **Buyer's remorse** - Changed your mind
- **Haven't contacted seller** - Always try seller first
- **Outside protection window** - Too much time has passed

## Before Opening a Dispute

1. **Contact the seller** - Try to resolve directly
2. **Give seller time** to respond (24-48 hours)
3. **Document the issue** - Take photos, save messages
4. **Be specific** - Explain exactly what's wrong

## How to Open a Dispute

1. **Go to your order** in "My Orders"
2. **Click "Open Dispute"**
3. **Select dispute reason**
4. **Provide details** and evidence
5. **Submit dispute**

## What Happens Next

- **Admin reviews** your dispute
- **Seller is notified** and can respond
- **Evidence is reviewed** from both sides
- **Decision is made** - Refund, partial refund, or other resolution

## Dispute Timeline

- **Response time**: Admin reviews within a few business days
- **Resolution time**: Varies by complexity
- **You'll be notified** of the decision

## Tips

- **Be honest and specific** in your dispute
- **Provide clear evidence** (photos, messages)
- **Respond promptly** if admin asks questions
- **Be patient** - Reviews take time
