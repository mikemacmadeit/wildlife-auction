# Providing Evidence for Disputes

Strong evidence helps resolve disputes quickly and fairly.

## Types of Evidence

### Photos
- **Show the problem** clearly
- **Multiple angles** if needed
- **Good lighting** for clarity
- **Comparison shots** (listing photo vs. received item)

### Messages
- **Screenshots** of conversations with seller
- **Email correspondence** if applicable
- **Show communication attempts** to resolve

### Documentation
- **Tracking information** (for delivery issues)
- **Receipts or invoices**
- **Expert opinions** (if applicable)
- **Other relevant documents**

## Photo Tips

**Do:**
- Use good lighting
- Show the problem clearly
- Include multiple angles
- Compare to listing photos
- Include timestamps if relevant

**Don't:**
- Use blurry or dark photos
- Only show minor issues
- Edit or manipulate photos
- Include personal information

## What to Document

**Item Not as Described:**
- Photos showing differences
- Listing photos for comparison
- Specific discrepancies noted

**Item Not Received:**
- Tracking information
- Delivery attempts/notices
- Communication with seller
- Proof of address issues (if applicable)

**Damaged Item:**
- Photos of damage
- Packaging photos (if damaged in transit)
- When damage was discovered
- Extent of damage

## Organizing Evidence

- **Label photos** clearly
- **Provide context** for each piece of evidence
- **Explain relevance** in your dispute description
- **Be organized** - easier for admin to review

## Best Practices

- **Be honest** - Don't exaggerate issues
- **Be specific** - Point out exact problems
- **Be timely** - Provide evidence promptly
- **Be complete** - Include all relevant information
