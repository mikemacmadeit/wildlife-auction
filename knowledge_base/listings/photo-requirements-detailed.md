# Photo Requirements and Best Practices

Complete guide to taking and uploading great photos for your listings.

## Photo Requirements

### Minimum Requirements

**Required:**
- At least 1 photo (required to publish)
- Photo must be clear and show the item
- Supported formats: JPG, JPEG, PNG
- Maximum file size: 10MB per photo
- Recommended: 3-5 photos minimum

### Technical Requirements

**File formats:**
- JPG/JPEG (most common)
- PNG (for transparency if needed)
- Other formats may not be supported

**File size:**
- Maximum: 10MB per photo
- Recommended: 1-3MB per photo
- Larger files may upload slowly
- Compress if needed

**Dimensions:**
- Minimum: 800x600 pixels
- Recommended: 1200x900 pixels or larger
- Square or landscape orientation
- High resolution for clarity

## Photo Quality Standards

### What Makes a Good Photo

**Quality characteristics:**
- **Clear and sharp** - Not blurry
- **Well-lit** - Good lighting, not too dark
- **In focus** - Item is clearly visible
- **Good composition** - Item is centered and framed well
- **Accurate colors** - Colors represent item correctly

### What Makes a Bad Photo

**Avoid:**
- Blurry or out of focus
- Too dark or too bright
- Cluttered background
- Item too small in frame
- Distorted or stretched
- Poor quality or pixelated

## Taking Great Photos

### Lighting

**Best lighting:**
- **Natural daylight** - Best option, take photos outside or near window
- **Even lighting** - Avoid harsh shadows
- **Bright but not direct sun** - Avoid glare
- **Multiple light sources** - Fill in shadows

**Lighting tips:**
- Take photos during daytime
- Use natural light when possible
- Avoid flash (can cause glare)
- Use diffused light
- Adjust for best visibility

### Composition

**Framing tips:**
- **Center the item** - Main subject in center
- **Fill the frame** - Item should be prominent
- **Show full item** - Don't cut off important parts
- **Multiple angles** - Front, side, back views
- **Close-ups** - Show important details

**Background:**
- **Simple and clean** - Uncluttered background
- **Neutral colors** - Don't distract from item
- **Consistent** - Similar backgrounds for all photos
- **Professional** - Clean, organized appearance

### For Animals

**What to photograph:**
- **Full body shots** - Front, side, back views
- **Head shots** - Clear face/muzzle photos
- **Markings** - Unique patterns or features
- **Condition** - Show health and condition
- **Paperwork** - Registration, health certificates
- **Action shots** - If applicable (riding, working)

**Tips:**
- Make sure animal is clean
- Good lighting on animal
- Animal should be calm and posed
- Show animal's best features
- Include multiple angles

### For Equipment

**What to photograph:**
- **All sides** - Front, back, left, right
- **Controls** - Dashboards, controls, features
- **Condition** - Show wear, damage, condition
- **Serial numbers** - If visible and relevant
- **Details** - Important features, attachments
- **In use** - If possible, show it working

**Tips:**
- Clean equipment before photos
- Good lighting on all sides
- Show condition honestly
- Highlight key features
- Include all important details

## Photo Organization

### First Photo (Main Image)

**First photo is most important:**
- This is the thumbnail buyers see first
- Should be your best photo
- Shows item clearly and attractively
- Represents item accurately
- Draws buyers in

**Best first photo:**
- Clear, well-lit full view
- Item looks its best
- Professional appearance
- Accurate representation
- Eye-catching but honest

### Photo Order

**Organize photos logically:**
1. **Main view** - Best overall shot
2. **Different angles** - Front, side, back
3. **Close-ups** - Important details
4. **Features** - Special characteristics
5. **Paperwork** - Documentation (if relevant)
6. **Condition** - Any wear or issues

## Common Photo Mistakes

### Mistakes to Avoid

**Don't:**
- Use old photos that don't match current condition
- Take photos in poor lighting
- Use blurry or low-quality images
- Show cluttered or messy backgrounds
- Cut off important parts of item
- Use filters that misrepresent item
- Take only one photo
- Skip important angles or details

### How to Fix Common Issues

**If photos are too dark:**
- Take photos during daylight
- Move to better lighting
- Use additional light sources
- Adjust camera settings

**If photos are blurry:**
- Hold camera steady
- Use proper focus
- Ensure good lighting
- Use higher resolution
- Check camera settings

**If file is too large:**
- Compress image
- Reduce resolution slightly
- Use image editing software
- Save as optimized JPG

## Uploading Photos

### Upload Process

**How to upload:**
1. Go to listing creation/edit page
2. Find photo upload section
3. Click "Upload" or drag and drop
4. Select photos from your device
5. Wait for upload to complete
6. Arrange photo order if needed
7. Add captions if desired (optional)

### Upload Tips

**Best practices:**
- Upload photos in order you want them
- Wait for each upload to complete
- Check photos appear correctly
- Verify all photos uploaded
- Test that photos are viewable

## Editing Photos

### Basic Editing

**You can edit:**
- Crop to improve composition
- Adjust brightness/contrast
- Rotate if needed
- Resize if too large
- Basic color correction

**Don't over-edit:**
- Don't misrepresent item
- Keep colors accurate
- Don't hide damage or issues
- Be honest in photos
- Show true condition

### Editing Tools

**Options:**
- Built-in photo editor (if available)
- Image editing software
- Online photo editors
- Mobile photo apps
- Basic adjustments only

## Photo Best Practices Summary

### Do's

✅ Take photos in good lighting
✅ Use multiple angles
✅ Show item clearly
✅ Include close-ups of details
✅ Use clean, simple backgrounds
✅ Take 5-10 photos minimum
✅ Make first photo your best
✅ Show item honestly

### Don'ts

❌ Use blurry or dark photos
❌ Take only one photo
❌ Use cluttered backgrounds
❌ Misrepresent item condition
❌ Skip important angles
❌ Use old or outdated photos
❌ Over-edit or filter photos
❌ Hide damage or issues
